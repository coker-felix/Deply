Malaria kills over 1,100 people yearly. Therefore, accurate laboratory diagnosis is essential, particularly to detect infections with the potentially fatal Plasmodium falciparum. The primary method of diagnosis of malaria in most clinical laboratories is still by the microscopic examination of thick and thin blood films. This requires the laboratorians to visually inspect the slides and based on their observation identify the specie of the parasite present. A growing demand for technologies that reduce the burden on laboratorians and support speedy decision-making has being realised.

Plas-AI, an AI-driven web app developed by Felix Coker, Salami Redeemer and Emmanuel A. Asiamah (Faculty Member of the University of Health and Allied Sciences, Ghana) is an easy to use Plasmodium speciation software for Malaria parasite Images.


NOTE: This app is solely designed as an entry to GADS2020 project phase and hence not licensed to be used in clinical settings. We strongly advise it's used under medical supervision.
